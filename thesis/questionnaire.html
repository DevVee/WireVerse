Direction: After using the WireVerse application, answer the following questions to measure what you have learned. Choose the letter of the best answer.

  1. According to the Philippine Electrical Code (PEC), the minimum wire size for lighting branch circuits is:
    1. AWG 16
    2. AWG 14
    3. AWG 12
    4. AWG 10
  2. Which of the following wire types is NOT recommended for permanent concealed wiring?
    1. THHN wire
    2. TW wire
    3. Extension cord wire
    4. UF (Underground Feeder) cable
  3. If a switch is incorrectly wired to the neutral wire instead of the hot wire, what is the consequence?
    1. The circuit works the same as a correct installation
    2. The device remains energized even when the switch is OFF, creating a shock hazard
    3. The circuit breaker trips immediately upon switch operation
    4. The device receives less power than normal
  4. The hot (live) wire color in standard Philippine residential wiring is:
    1. White
    2. Green
    3. Black or Red
    4. Yellow
  5. A 20-ampere circuit requires a minimum wire gauge of:
    1. AWG 14
    2. AWG 12
    3. AWG 10
    4. AWG 8
  6. Which of the following correctly describes a series circuit?
    1. All devices share the same voltage regardless of resistance
    2. Current flows through one single path, passing through all devices in sequence
    3. Each device has its own independent current path
    4. The circuit only uses direct current (DC)
  7. What is the correct procedure for stripping wire insulation safely?
    1. Use a utility knife and cut deeply around the insulation
    2. Set the wire stripper to the correct gauge, clamp, and pull the insulation off
    3. Use long-nose pliers to peel the insulation by hand
    4. Burn the insulation off with a lighter
  8. When installing a duplex outlet, the neutral wire connects to the:
    1. Smaller (narrow) slot terminal — brass-colored screw
    2. Larger (wide) slot terminal — silver-colored screw
    3. Grounding (round hole) terminal — green screw
    4. Either slot terminal interchangeably
  9. What does the ampere rating of a circuit breaker indicate?
    1. The maximum voltage the breaker can handle
    2. The maximum continuous current the circuit can carry before the breaker trips
    3. The resistance of the wiring in the circuit
    4. The power in watts that the circuit can produce
  10. In an outlet installation, the grounding wire is connected to the:
    1. Brass-colored terminal screw
    2. Silver-colored terminal screw
    3. Green terminal screw (U-shaped slot)
    4. No terminal — it is left loose
  11. According to safety standards, what is the recommended mounting height of a standard wall outlet from the finished floor level in the Philippines?
    1. 6 inches (15 cm)
    2. 12 inches (30 cm)
    3. 18 inches (45 cm)
    4. 36 inches (90 cm)
  12. What does Ohm's Law state?
    1. Voltage equals Current divided by Resistance (V = I / R)
    2. Current equals Voltage multiplied by Resistance (I = V × R)
    3. Voltage equals Current multiplied by Resistance (V = I × R)
    4. Resistance equals Voltage multiplied by Current (R = V × I)
  13. A circuit with a short circuit is characterized by:
    1. A very high resistance path that reduces current
    2. A very low resistance unintended path that causes excessive current flow
    3. An open path where no current can flow
    4. A circuit that is too long for its breaker rating
  14. What is the purpose of using an electrical conduit in wiring installations?
    1. To increase the current capacity of the wire
    2. To reduce voltage drop across long wire runs
    3. To protect wires from physical damage and provide a raceway system
    4. To insulate the wire from moisture only
  15. After completing a wiring installation, what should be done before energizing the circuit?
    1. Test all connections with a continuity tester and verify proper polarity
    2. Immediately switch on the circuit breaker to check if it trips
    3. Visually inspect only and proceed without testing
    4. Ask someone else to hold the wires while you turn the breaker on
